Origami Jump

Iowa State University: VRAC

UI design and 3D modeling

Spring 2023


Project summary

Origami Jump is an educational video game that introduces players to the concept of DNA origami‚Äîa process where scientists fold strands of DNA into specific shapes at the nanoscale. The goal was to make a topic that‚Äôs usually reserved for research papers feel exciting and approachable.


This game is currently being used for a Human Computer Interaction thesis at Iowa State University to investigate the differences between learning in game formats and lecture formats. I will post the link to the completed research here once it‚Äôs complete so be sure to check back in!


My role

Working with researchers from Iowa State University‚Äôs Human Computer Interaction program, I designed a UI meets the educational and gameplay goals of the project while also adding visual interest and keeping players engaged in the puzzle tasks. I also created game-ready 3D assets that supported the game‚Äôs world design and learning objectives.


The challenge

The challenge of this UI development was to find a way to effectively teach the basic verbs of DNA origami to the player, in a way that would be comparable to the lecture and quiz that other participants would take. The UI teaches two main concepts, DNA synthesis and annealing.


I created a UI that mimics the real world processes of DNA synthesis by using a test tube graphic to represent the area where players synthesize individual nucleotides together, mimicking the real world laboratory environment.


Annealing is the main way players will be interacting with the game environment. Players build bridges by annealing DNA strands they built using synthesis to matching strands around them.


You can check out the detailed project brief: here.


Solution

To create the look and feel of the UI, I conducted a market analysis to study existing educational games. Specifically, I looked at how those games taught and gamified complex ideas, how they structured player progression, and what kinds of visual styles were effective in an educational context as well as achievable for our small development team. This research was used to direct both the UI and mechanics of the final game.


You can take a look at the market research document here.


I also created a collection of concept sketches for 3D assets that began to communicate the goals, world, emotions, and lessons of the game to the player. These sketches guided the look and feel of the final 3D models for the game.


You can view the concept sketches I made: here.


Results: UI Prototype

The final prototype included a working UI that let players collect nucleotides, synthesize them into sequences, and use those sequences to anneal with strands in the game world. It was intuitive enough for new players to pick up quickly, but allowed for players to learn the major verbs of DNA origami, synthesizing and annealing.
