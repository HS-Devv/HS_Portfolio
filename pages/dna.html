Origami Jump

Background

Origami Jump is an educational videogame that teaches students about the concept of DNA origami, the process for folding and manipulating DNA nucleotides into specific shapes.


Process

Creating an educational video game requires a lot of research! For this project, I collaborated with the head researcher and professors in Iowa State University's Human Computer Interaction program to create the design requirements and recommendations on how to best create an interface that could educate the player while remaining immersive and, above all, usable.


The most enjoyable part of the design process was playing and researching existing educational games! I looked for games with exciting visual styles, educational lessons, and examples of gamified learning progress.


I also created a collection of concept sketches for 3D assets that could communicate the goals, world, emotions, and lessons of the game to the player in a fun and engaging way.
